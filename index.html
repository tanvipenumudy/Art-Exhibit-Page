Art

Art has been a part of our life for as long as humanity has existed. For thousands of years people have been creating, looking at, criticizing, and enjoying art. I would like to address three questions: what is art, what is its purpose, and why has it survived for this long.

Art
No. 1

First, what is art? Humanity has faced this question for many centuries. How can we distinguish between fine art and a beautiful poster?

Art
No. 2

How can we call both Malevich's "Black Square" and Da Vinci's "La Joconde" art, yet not include some people's paintings into this category?

Art
No. 3

I believe that the only things that distinguishes these works are the artist and the artist's objective that is initially taken up.

Art
No. 4

The connection between what the artist means to achieve and what he achieves through the medium is what I believe classifies art.

Art
No. 5

If what the artist meant to say is understood by at least one person in this world, when we can safely call that piece art.

Art
No. 6

What classifies a masterpiece, however, is when not only one person understands what the artist was trying to say.
